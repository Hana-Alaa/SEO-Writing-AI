1. CTA PHILOSOPHY (FOR COMMERCIAL INTENT):
   - Treat every CTA like a human recommendation, not a marketing push.
   - Ask yourself: "What would a reader who just learned this fact naturally do next?"
   - Write the CTA as the logical continuation of that thought, integrated into the sentence.
   - For commercial sections, you must include at least TWO such moments of natural recommendation.
   - The first paragraph of any non-introduction section must introduce context first — no CTA there.
   - Urgency is only appropriate if the brand's actual business has real scarcity (limited seats, event dates, deadlines).
   - Sales intensity guide: high = confident + urgent; medium = helpful + calm; low = purely informative.


2. Links:
   A. Internal Links:
       - Use only URLs provided in 'Mandatory Internal Links'. Maximum TWO per section.
       - In the Introduction, use the Brand URL if available.
       - Anchor text must describe the actual value of the destination, not a generic label.
       - Do NOT repeat a URL already in 'Used Internal Links'.
   
   B. External Links:
       - FOLLOW RULE 6 in the base prompt for external linking behavior.
       - Format as natural inline markdown: [Catchy Descriptive Title](https://actual-url.com)

6. FAQ behavior:
   - **Useful & Brand-Focused**: Every FAQ answer must be helpful to the user AND naturally highlight a strength of the brand's service.
   - Address exact pain points, Pricing/Safety concerns, or "How to" steps that position the brand as the solution.
   - Use "Objection Handling" style: predict what the user is afraid of or confused by, and clarify it confidently.

7. Conclusion Behavior:
   - If section_type = conclusion, the final paragraph MUST NOT be a weak motivational sign-off.
   - Instead, it MUST culminate in a high-urgency, conversion-focused directive.
   - Follow the strict anchor text rule (highly descriptive, benefit-driven).
